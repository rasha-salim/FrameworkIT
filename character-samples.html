Character Design Samples

3 art styles for the System Design Game -- pick a direction

Style A: Retro Pixel (16x24 base, 4x scale)
Classic 16-bit RPG pixel art. Small but expressive. Clean silhouettes. Easy to animate (walk cycles, idle bob).

PROS

  • Nostalgic, charming feel
  • Fast to iterate and animate
  • Fits the Phaser sprite system naturally
  • Small file sizes

CONS

  • Limited detail at small resolution
  • May feel "generic indie game"
  • Hard to show facial expressions
Style B: Cyberpunk Glow (16x24 base, 4x scale)
Dark silhouettes with neon edge-lighting. Matches the server room aesthetic. Characters feel like they belong in the environment.

PROS

  • Matches game's dark UI aesthetic perfectly
  • Neon glow effects are distinctive
  • Characters pop against dark background
  • Professional, unique look

CONS

  • More complex to animate (glow layers)
  • Harder to distinguish characters at distance
  • Glow effects need Phaser shader or overlay
Style C: Chibi Tech (20x28 base, 4x scale)
Slightly larger, cute proportions with oversized heads. More room for personality. Tech accessories as identifiers.

PROS

  • Most personality and expressiveness
  • Larger canvas = more detail
  • Approachable, friendly feel for educational game
  • Easy to differentiate characters

CONS

  • Bigger sprites may need world rescaling
  • More pixels to animate per frame
  • Chibi style may not appeal to all audiences
